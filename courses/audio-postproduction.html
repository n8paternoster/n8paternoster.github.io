Audio Postproduction

Audio Postproduction is an introductory course to film and audio/video technologies. Topics covered include sound design, digital media tools, video editing, and manipulation of audio/visual material. We covered a brief history and analysis of film standards and how they evolved into digital technologies. The projects throughout the semester focused on sound design using MIDI technology and occasionally Foley to create believable sound effects for well-known movie scenes. The final project was a compilation of these short clips set to a "DVD-style" menu that we created and animated.

Projects

The sounds I used in these projects came from one of three main sources. I used pre-recorded sound effects from several popular sound effect libraries, MIDI sounds I created myself using synthesizers, and live Foley sounds I recorded myself. Every sound I used went through heavy editing to shape it to the movie and scene. Some of my more utilized edits and effects included volume control, panning, EQ filtering, reverb, tremolo, and distortion. I automated many of these effects to change over time in accordance with the action on screen. The most enjoyable sounds of all were the Foley effects I came up with and recorded. It's pretty fun to try to find just the right sound by hitting various things together.

  • Rancor Battle from Episode VI: Return of the Jedi

  • Millennium Falcon from Episode IV: A New Hope

  • Final Project - An Animated Intro and Menu

    The final project combined the rest of the semester's projects into an animated menu designed to showcase our efforts. This project consisted entirely of creating and animating the menu. The individual sound design projects were done in Logic Pro and the animation was created with Apple Motion.
